# Platform Derivation
**Document ID:** `06_Marketing/Platform_Derivation.md`
**Referenced by:** `06_Marketing/README.md` (Knowledge Base, Workflows steps 3–5)

> Per-platform format, framing, and tone rules. All derivations pull from the same source video (`05_Production`) — this file governs *how* the same content is reshaped, not what content exists.

## YouTube Shorts
- **Format:** 9:16 vertical, typically ≤60 seconds.
- **Role (Constitution §7):** top-of-funnel discovery, feeding the primary long-form video.
- **Framing:** re-crop to follow the on-screen action/speaker, not a fixed center-crop of the 16:9 source — if the source segment is PiP (avatar + tool UI), prioritize whichever element the spoken content is actually referencing at each moment.
- **Hook:** must land in the first 1–3 seconds — tighter than the long-form 5–8 second window (`01_Core/Quality_Standards.md`), since vertical feeds reward near-instant retention.
- **CTA:** typically points back to the full video, not a generic subscribe ask.

## Instagram Reels
- **Format:** 9:16 vertical, similar length norms to Shorts.
- **Role (Constitution §7):** discovery and brand presence.
- **Framing:** same re-crop principle as Shorts.
- **Hook:** same fast-attention window as Shorts.
- **Tone:** within the persona's energy-level range (`System/Shima_Persona.md` §2), Reels can sit at the higher end of that range, consistent with the platform's faster pacing norms — this is an energy adjustment only, never a core-voice change (`01_Core/Quality_Standards.md`).

## TikTok
- **Format:** 9:16 vertical.
- **Role (Constitution §7):** discovery, skews younger audience.
- **Framing:** same re-crop principle as Shorts/Reels.
- **Tone:** slightly more casual framing is acceptable within the persona's defined energy range for this platform's audience — still bounded by whatever formality register `System/Shima_Persona.md` §2 eventually locks in; until that's resolved, this stays close to the other short-form platforms rather than diverging further.

## Telegram
- **Format:** text-forward post; may include the full video, a derived clip, or no video at all — no fixed aspect-ratio constraint.
- **Role (Constitution §7):** owned-audience channel, highest-trust distribution, least platform-algorithm risk.
- **Content shape:** a summary or direct quote of the video's core claim, written with more room for nuance and caveats than a short-form caption allows — this is where the News category's Nuance/What's Next section, for example, can be represented in full rather than compressed to a single line.
- **Framing:** not applicable in the video-reframing sense; the "framing" decision here is textual — how much context to include, not how to crop.
