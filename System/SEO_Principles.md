# SEO Principles
**Document ID:** `System/SEO_Principles.md`
**Referenced by:** `07_SEO/README.md`

> Distinct from `07_SEO/README.md` (the engine's process — how titles/descriptions/tags/chapters/thumbnails actually get produced): this file is the underlying philosophy that process serves.

## Build for retention, not just clicks

A title or thumbnail's job is to earn an honest click from the viewer most likely to actually want the video — not to maximize click volume from anyone. `07_SEO/README.md`'s honesty rule (a title/thumbnail may not claim more than the video delivers) is the mechanical enforcement of this; the reasoning is that a click that ends in an immediate bounce (because the title overpromised) damages the channel's standing with platform algorithms more than a slightly lower click-through rate from an honest title does. Retention is the metric that compounds; raw clicks don't.

## Keyword strategy serves the viewer, not the algorithm

Tags and description keywords are chosen because they genuinely describe what's in the video and what a real viewer would search for — not stuffed to catch tangential traffic. `07_SEO/README.md`'s Decision Rules already state the comparison-keyword rule (a competitor's name is a legitimate tag only if the video actually discusses it); this principle generalizes that to every keyword decision.

## Thumbnail-title pairing

Per `DECISIONS.md` D-018, thumbnails live in `07_SEO`. The two are designed as a pair, not independently: the title and thumbnail together should communicate the video's actual angle (from `03_IdeaEngine`'s brief) at a glance, without either one needing the other to avoid being misleading on its own.
